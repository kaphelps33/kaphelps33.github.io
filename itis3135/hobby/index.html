Home

A synthesizer, or sometimes just called a synth, is an electronic musical instrument that generates sounds via waveforms.

The synthesizer is one of the most important instruments in the history of music, still being used in every genre of music today.

the only innovation that can stand alongside the electric guitar as a great new instrument of the age of electricity ... Both led to new forms of music, and both had massive popular appeal. - Analog Days
The synthesizer is as important, and as ubiquitous, in modern music today as the human voice. - Fact, 2016

What

A synthesizer is an electronic device that generates sound through the manipulation of electrical signals. This process can be understood scientifically through the lens of waveforms, signal processing, and acoustics.

At its core, sound is a vibration that travels through a medium (like air) and is interpreted by our ears as pressure waves. These waves can be represented mathematically as functions of time, often using sine and cosine functions to describe the waveforms. A synthesizer takes advantage of this by generating and manipulating electrical signals to create a wide range of sounds. These signals can then be amplified and converted into sound waves by a speaker.

When

Synthesizers the instrument of yesterday, today, and tomorrow

1897: The Telharmonium

The Telharmonium, invented by Thaddeus Cahill, was the first electronic instrument and a precursor to the synthesizer. This large, complex machine created sound by generating electrical signals and converting them into sound through telephone receivers.

1920s: The Theremin

Invented by Russian physicist Leon Theremin, the Theremin was an early electronic instrument controlled without physical contact. It generated eerie, continuous tones and became popular in experimental and film music.

1964: The Moog Synthesizer

Dr. Robert Moog introduced the Moog synthesizer, a modular, analog instrument that offered musicians the ability to shape sounds with oscillators, filters, and envelope controllers. Its portability and ease of use helped bring synthesizers into mainstream music.

1970s: Polyphonic Synthesizers

As synthesizer technology advanced, companies like Yamaha and Oberheim introduced polyphonic synthesizers, allowing musicians to play multiple notes at once. This development was pivotal for the instrument's use in pop and rock music.

1980s: The Digital Revolution

The Yamaha DX7, the first commercially successful digital synthesizer, brought FM synthesis to the masses. Digital synthesizers became the foundation of 1980s pop music, leading to iconic sounds in genres from new wave to synthpop.

1990s-Present: Virtual and Software Synthesizers

With the rise of powerful computers, virtual synthesizers emerged, allowing musicians to create synthesized sounds using software. Today, synthesizers are integral to music across genres, available in both hardware and software forms.

Where

Where to find, experience, and learn about synthesizers

Learning

To learn about synthesizers you must first learn about synthesis. A great, modern resource for this is Syntorial, a website that teaches you to make synth patches by ear.

Your first synth

Your first synth should probably free, digital one instead of a $2,000+, 30lbs analog beast. Here are some that I can recommend:

  • Vital - This is a digital wavetable synth that will give you digital sounds.
  • OB-Xd - This is an analog emulation of the Oberheim OB-X

Growing up

Once you have spent some time mastering the free tools, you can begin to look into the high quality stuff such as:

  • Serum - A fantastic, industry staple that will never steer you wrong.
  • Diva - A beautiful analog modeling synth that creates the most amazing sounds you can get out of a computer.
  • All of Arturia - The V collection is so high quality its best to wait until its on sale and get the whole pack for under $100. You have thousands of hours worth of stuff just in this pack alone.

Lets get physical

This is where you start getting into the physical synth hardware. Your research should be like your budget, high.

Analog Synthesizers
  • Sequential OB-6 - A classic analog sound with a modern interface, around $2,999.
  • Moog Subsequent 37 - Known for thick, warm bass and leads, priced at around $1,699.
  • Behringer Poly D - A budget-friendly option inspired by the Moog Model D, around $799.
Digital Synthesizers
  • Roland JD-XA - A hybrid synth with both analog and digital engines, priced around $2,499.
  • Yamaha Montage 8 - Feature-packed, with a focus on performance and depth, at around $4,299.
  • Korg Modwave - A modern digital synth inspired by wavetable synthesis, priced around $799.
Wavetable Synthesizers
  • ASM Hydrasynth - Excellent modulation and sound design capabilities, at around $1,299.
  • Elektron Digitone - A unique digital synth with FM and wavetable capabilities, around $899.
  • Modal Electronics Argon8 - Versatile, well-priced wavetable synth, at around $849.

Going Vintage

If you're looking for iconic sounds, vintage synths deliver, but they come at a premium. These classics are both pricey and often tricky to maintain. Here are some of the most sought-after vintage synths:

  • Roland Jupiter-8 - Known for lush, full sounds, priced around $10,000+ if you can find one in good condition.
  • Oberheim OB-Xa - Another classic used in countless 80s hits, usually going for around $7,000.

Grailed Synths

These are the holy grails of the synth world. Often rare or highly modular, they are known for their unique capabilities and are loved by serious sound designers.

  • Yamaha CS-80 - A legendary analog synth made famous by Vangelis. Ever seen Blade Runner? This is essentially the whole movie, with prices starting at around $60,000+.
  • Moog Model 55 Modular System - A reissued modular synth inspired by the 1970s classic, priced around $35,000.
  • Arp 2600c Grey Marvin 1971 - Only 34 ever made, modular, need I say more? Priced around $60,000

This list only scratches the surface of the vast world of synthesizers. Each type, whether analog, digital, wavetable, or modular, offers an incredible range of sounds and functionality, with endless possibilities for experimentation and creativity. From boutique brands to vintage legends, the choices are nearly limitless, and new innovations are constantly shaping the synth landscape. If you choose to go deeper, you’ll discover an almost endless amount (especially if you go modular) of synth types, effects, and modular components that can take your music to the most beautiful or unchained places. There’s always something new to explore in the world of synthesis.

How

How to Get the Most Out of Your Synthesizers

Assumptions

This super brief guide is written in the hopes that you already have:

  1. Listed to synth music (be that '80s, synthwave, etc...) ever.
  2. Have at least some understanding of music theory.
  3. Possibly have experience playing the piano (will cut learning time in half).
  4. Have a synth!

Avoid GAS!

Gear Acquisition Syndrome (GAS) is a common phenomenon among musicians and synth enthusiasts, characterized by an unquenchable thirst to acquire more equipment. While it's natural to want the latest synths and gear, constantly chasing new items can lead to financial strain and distract from your primary goal: making music. To combat GAS, start by focusing on mastering the tools you currently have. Take the time to explore all the features of your synths and experiment with sound design, rather than immediately looking to purchase the next creativity and expression, not just accumulating equipment. GAS can ruin the musical experience and has for a lot of people. Don't be one of them!

Understanding Signal Flow

Before diving into sound design, it's crucial to grasp the basics of signal flow. Understanding how sound travels through the different modules of your synth—oscillators, filters, envelopes, and effects—will empower you to create unique sounds. Start with simple patches and gradually explore more complex routing to develop your skills. (Refer to my writings above about learning synthesis)

Experimenting with Patches

Don’t hesitate to tweak presets and experiment with different parameters. Try adjusting oscillator waveforms, filter cutoff, and modulation settings to see how they affect the sound. Remember, some of the best sounds come from unexpected settings!

Layering Sounds

One effective technique in synthesizer sound design is layering multiple sounds together. This could involve using two different synths or layering multiple instances of the same synth. Experiment with slightly detuning them or using different waveforms to create a richer, fuller sound.

Using MIDI Effectively

MIDI is a powerful tool when it comes to controlling your synthesizers. Learn to use MIDI controllers and sequencers to manipulate your sounds. You can automate parameters and create intricate patterns that add depth to your compositions.

Exploring Effects

Don't overlook the impact of effects like reverb, delay, and distortion. These can dramatically change the character of your sound. Invest time in learning how to effectively use these effects within your signal chain to enhance your overall sound design.

Joining Communities

Engaging with online communities can significantly enhance your synthesizer journey. Platforms like Reddit's r/synthesizers and forums such as Gearspace are great places to share your experiences, ask for advice, and discover new techniques from fellow enthusiasts.

Keeping Up with Trends

The synthesizer world is always evolving, with new gear and techniques emerging regularly. Follow blogs, YouTube channels, and social media influencers in the synth community to stay informed about the latest trends and innovations.

Experimenting with Different Genres

Don’t limit yourself to one style of music. Experiment with synthesizers across various genres, from electronic to ambient, to discover new sounds and techniques that inspire you. Each genre has its unique approach to sound design that can enhance your creativity.

Avoiding Snobbery

So many people get so caught up in their synth "camps" and how they do things that they ruin it for everyone else. Music is subjective, do what you want to do and what makes you happy. So many famous songs that inspire everyone today were made using unorthodox techniques. Don't get so caught up in how its supposed to be done that you forget why you do it in the first place.

Why

The Importance of Synthesizers in Music

Synthesizers have revolutionized the music landscape, providing artists with endless creative possibilities. They enable musicians to explore unique soundscapes and push the boundaries of traditional music genres. Whether you're looking to create atmospheric pads, punchy basslines, or intricate melodies, synthesizers offer a versatile toolkit for sonic experimentation.

Creativity and Expression

At their core, synthesizers empower musicians to express their individuality. By manipulating waveforms, filters, and modulation, artists can craft sounds that resonate with their emotions and ideas. This level of control encourages experimentation, leading to fresh and innovative compositions.

Inclusion in Modern Music

Today, synthesizers are integral to various music styles, from pop and electronic to hip-hop and film scoring. They not only enhance production quality but also add a distinct character to tracks, making them more engaging and memorable. The presence of synths in popular music has opened doors for new genres and collaborations, bridging the gap between technology and artistry.

Building a Community

Engaging with synthesizers fosters a sense of community among musicians and enthusiasts. Whether through online forums, social media, or local meetups, sharing knowledge, tips, and experiences can inspire growth and collaboration. This network of like-minded individuals can be invaluable in your musical journey.

In conclusion, synthesizers are not just instruments; they are powerful tools that enhance creativity, shape modern music, and connect people. Whether you're a seasoned musician or just starting, exploring synthesizers can unlock new levels of artistic expression and enjoyment.
